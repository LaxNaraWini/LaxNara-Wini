Starting from Zero — My Creator Journey as a Beginner

A honest and personal story about building something meaningful while juggling college, a part time job, and everything else life throws at you.

I am just a beginner and that is okay

I want to start this by being completely honest with you because I think the internet already has enough highlight reels and perfectly curated success stories, and what it actually needs more of is real people sharing what it looks like to start from absolutely nothing and keep going anyway. So here is my truth: I have been building LaxNar Studio for three months and I am still very much a beginner who is figuring things out one day at a time, and I am writing this because I genuinely believe that my journey might be exactly what someone out there needs to read right now.

What my life actually looks like behind the scenes

If you are imagining that I sit in a beautiful organized space with unlimited time to pour into this creative business, I want you to know that the reality is a little different. I am a college student which already takes up a significant portion of my time and energy, and on top of that I also work a part time job to support myself. LaxNar Studio is something I build in the pockets of time that are left over after all of that, which means late nights, early mornings, and weekends spent creating content, designing products, writing blogs, and learning everything I can about how to grow something from scratch with very little resources and zero guarantees.

The hardest part by far has been staying consistent through all of it because consistency sounds so simple when people talk about it online but it is genuinely one of the most challenging things to maintain when you are tired, when nothing seems to be working yet, and when the people around you might not fully understand why you are spending so much time on something that has not made money yet.

The days when I almost gave up

There have been days when I looked at everything I was trying to do and felt completely overwhelmed by how much there still was to figure out and how slow the progress seemed to be moving. There were moments where I wondered if I was wasting my time, if anyone would ever actually care about what I was creating, and if it would ever amount to anything real. I think anybody who is honest about their journey will tell you that those moments exist, and that they visit you a lot more often in the beginning than you ever expected them to when you first started out with all that initial excitement and hope.

But here is what I kept coming back to every single time doubt tried to make me quit: I trust the universe and I know it has my back. That might sound simple but for me it is the most grounding thing I have, this deep knowing that if I keep showing up, keep creating, and keep putting genuine love and intention into everything I do, the right people will find it and something beautiful will grow from it in time. That belief has carried me through every hard day so far and I do not think I would still be here without it.

What I want you to take away from this

If you are also someone who is building something on the side while managing a full life of responsibilities, classes, work, family, and everything else, I want you to know that you are not behind and you are not doing it wrong just because progress feels slow. The fact that you are still showing up despite all of that is genuinely one of the most impressive things a person can do and it deserves to be celebrated even when the results are not visible yet.

Please do not give up just because it is taking longer than you thought it would. Please do not compare your chapter one to someone else's chapter ten. Please do not let the hard days convince you that the dream is not worth it, because it absolutely is and so are you. Every small thing you do, every product you create, every post you write, every day you choose to keep going is adding up to something real even when you cannot see it yet.

You do not have to have it all figured out to keep going. You just have to trust the process and refuse to give up on yourself.

Three months in and I am still here, still learning, still creating, and still believing that LaxNar Studio is going to become everything I know it can be. And I hope that wherever you are in your own journey right now, you choose to keep going too because the world genuinely needs what you are building. 🌸

With so much love, LaxNar Studio 💗
